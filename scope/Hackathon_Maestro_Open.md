# Hackathon Maestro Open

<aside>

To become the biggest school in the world, we need a product anyone, anywhere, on any device can use.

Your mission: build an [**OPEN**](https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/dd536b94-3bc6-482d-8d3a-fd301366d5f8) version of Maestro that reaches as many users as possible — at **$0 COGS** (or close to it).

</aside>

- Goals
    - End to end product experience
    - COGS: $0 (or close to it)
    - At scale
    - Engaging
- Brief
    
    **Your mission** — Build an open version of Maestro, end to end, that reaches as many users as possible at $0 cost per user (or close to it). A real product someone can open, learn from, and come back to. Not a feature, not a demo — a whole thing that works on its own.
    
    **Build a product people love** — You know Maestro from the inside — what works, what doesn't. Start simple, then iterate. The bar: enjoyable, efficient, and $0 COGS (that last one is the real challenge).
    
    **The one rule** — It can't cost us money when a new user shows up. No cloud LLM bill per lesson, no per-user server. The model runs on the user's own device — that's what makes it free to scale.
    
    **Tools you can use:**
    
    - Maestro's lesson structure — use the existing lesson format from Maestro/LMS; don't invent your own content model.
    - web-llm ([github.com/mlc-ai/web-llm](http://github.com/mlc-ai/web-llm)) — the tutor runs on the user's own hardware, no server inference.
    
    **Everything else is up to you** — What you build and how it reaches people is your call. Build it end to end. A simple product that fully works beats a pile of half-features.
    
    **Format** — Solo. You build it, you own it, you demo it.
    
    **What you show at the end** — A working product someone can use today, and a 10-minute presentation of what you built.
